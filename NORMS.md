# Community Norms

This project is licensed under the Apache License 2.0, which permits broad use including commercial use, modification, and redistribution.

The Apache License 2.0 does not contain a field-of-use restriction. We have chosen it deliberately because we believe in open knowledge and OSI-aligned licensing for the patterns we publish here.

That said, we have a request.

## What we ask

We respectfully ask members of the community **not to use this code to build a competing AI-discourse publication** — that is, not to operate a service or publication that generates AI-written analysis of public AI discourse for general audiences.

This is a community norm, not a legal restriction. We will not enforce it through litigation. We are asking, not requiring.

## What is welcome

The following uses are all welcome and encouraged:

- **Academic research and citation** — study the patterns, cite them, build on them in your own research.
- **Journalism and investigative use** — use the architecture and methodology in journalistic projects, including paid journalism.
- **Education and teaching** — use this code in courses, workshops, tutorials.
- **Personal and hobby projects** — fork and experiment.
- **Infrastructure adjacencies** — use the patterns in unrelated AI tools, data pipelines, or media systems.
- **Unrelated commercial work** — use the patterns in your commercial project, as long as it isn't a competing AI-discourse publication.
- **Contributions back** — open issues, send pull requests, propose improvements.

## When to ask

If you are uncertain whether your use case falls within the norm, please open an issue or email hello@aidran.ai before you start. We will engage in good faith.

## Why we ask this way

A license with a "no competing use" clause would not be OSI-approved, and we want to engage with the academic and open-source communities that care about that distinction. A non-binding norm preserves that engagement while making our preference explicit.

Most violations of community norms in open source are addressed reputationally rather than legally. We trust the community.
